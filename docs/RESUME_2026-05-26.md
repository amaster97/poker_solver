### 2. v1.8 SIMD speedup NOT validated empirically

The v1.8.0 release-notes draft (`docs/v1_8_0_release_notes_DRAFT.md` on branch `pr-88-v1.8.0-release-notes-prep`) claims **"~4-8× on Apple Silicon (M-series), ~2-4× on x86_64+AVX2, ~1.5-2× SSE2."** A bench agent measured end-to-end performance:

| Workload | v1.7.0 | post-Phase-3 main | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| River R-v-R, 1081 hands, 3 actions, 5 iter | 936 ms/iter | 942 ms/iter | **0.99×** |
| River R-v-R, 1081 hands, 5 actions, 5 iter | 4777 ms/iter | 4723 ms/iter | **1.01×** |
| Leduc, 288 infosets, 500 iter | 2212 µs/iter | 2237 µs/iter | **0.99×** |
| Kuhn, 12 infosets, 500 iter | 9.4 µs/iter | 9.8 µs/iter | **0.96×** |

- Full report: `/Users/ashen/Desktop/poker_solver/docs/v1_8_simd_perf_benchmark_2026-05-26.md`.
- **Root cause:** LLVM's `-O3` autovectorizer already covers the small-slice case on M4 Pro arm64; explicit-intrinsics dispatch overhead cancels the kernel-level win at typical action-count widths (2-5). Kernel microbenches do show 2-3× wins at width=8 but production calls are width 2-5.
- **Implication:** the release-notes draft's "4-8×" headline language is empirically WRONG on the hardware tested. The audit recommends rewording to "no measurable wall-clock improvement on M4 Pro for typical postflop widths; SIMD provides a portability + correctness floor (runtime-detected AVX2 dispatch, bit-identical scalar fallback)." Phase 4 not measured (was not on main at bench time; now it is).
- **What to decide:** approve the honest reframe (recommended), or keep the optimistic projection and frame it as "target not yet locked, pending Phase 4 + x86 measurement." The draft itself flagged the risk per `feedback_no_extrapolate`.
